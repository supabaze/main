Supported frameworks

CDP

CDP is a global non-profit that manages the world’s leading voluntary environmental disclosure system—covering climate, water, forests, biodiversity, and plastics. In 2024, over 24,800 entities, including more than 22,700 companies, submitted data that informs investors and supply chains on environmental risks and drives action toward a sustainable economy

Learn more →

EcoVadis

EcoVadis provides the world’s largest collaborative ESG rating platform, assessing over 150,000 organizations across 250+ industries using a methodology built on policies, actions, and results across environmental, social, ethics, and procurement themes. In 2023–24, it rated more than 150,000 companies and supported around €1.7 trillion in procurement spend.

Learn more →

GHG Protocol

Launched in 1998 by WRI and WBCSD, the GHG Protocol offers the world’s most widely used framework for measuring and reporting greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3), supporting organizations in building inventories that are robust, comparative, and transparent.

Learn more →

IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards (IFRS S1 & S2 / ISSB)

Issued by the ISSB in June 2023, IFRS S1 (general) and S2 (climate) create a global baseline for integrating material sustainability-related financial disclosures into mainstream reporting—aligned with TCFD pillars and intended for jurisdictions worldwide from January 1, 2024 onward.

Learn more →

Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi)

The SBTi is a global corporate climate-action partnership (including WRI, CDP, WWF, UNGC) that enables companies and financial institutions to set emissions reduction targets grounded in climate science—aligned with a 1.5 °C pathway and net-zero by 2050. Over 8,200 companies have had targets validated, with nearly 11,000 commitments to date

Learn more →
